Droughts of California visualized with open data

California is experiencing the worst drought in recorded history.

2014 California Drought

In 2014, California has received only 20% of its normal precipitation and had only 18% of its normal snow pack, and has faced a devastating three years of drought. Right now, 100% of California faces severe drought conditions according to the National Drought Mitigation Center.(citation #)

Drought, snowpack, and reservoir storage

Snowpack in California is the primary source of water to reservoirs that serve drinking water, agriculture, and hydroelectric needs.

For any given year, less wintertime snowpack results in lower reservoir levels, and less water for consumptive use.

Reservoir Storage

Drought reduces snowpack and results in lower reservoir storage capacity.

Over 80% of public drinking water supply comes from rivers, lakes, reservoirs, and other surface waters. (citation#)

Here, the effect of drought on 91 of California's >700 reservoirs are shown through time.

Drought and Fire

Wildfire is a natural part of the ecosystem in California. Drought years tend to have greater frequency and intensity of wildfires. Wet years promote the buildup of fuels, which then increases the ignitability fires in dryer years.

 

2000-2010 Average annual Impacts in California ($2011):

  • Number of fires: 5,000
  • Average annual damage: 177 million
  • Average acreage burned: 1.8 million
  • Structures destroyed: 1,100

Drought and Food Prices

Of consumptive water use, agriculture represents 74% of freshwater use (citation#)(Kenny et al., 2009)

For the first time in history, some agricultural areas are not receiving any water for irrigation which is resulting in devastation of historically productive areas.

The agricultural output for the state could fall by 3.5$ billion dollars this year
