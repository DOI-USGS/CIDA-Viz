Droughts of California visualized with open data

California is experiencing the worst drought in recorded history.

2014 California Drought

In 2014, California has received only 20% of its normal precipitation and had only 18% of its normal snow pack, and has faced a devastating three years of drought. Right now, 100% of California faces severe drought conditions according to the National Drought Mitigation Center.

Snowpack

Snowpack in California is the primary source of water to reservoirs that serve drinking water, agriculture and hydroelectric needs.

Reservoir Storage

Drought reduces snowpack and results in lower reservoir storage and capacity. For the first time in history, some agricultural areas are not receiving any water for irrigation which is resulting in devastation of historically productive areas. The agricultural output for the state could fall by 3.5$ billion dollars this year.

Wildfires

As of September 19, California has eight large, active wildfires spread over an area of 205 acres and growing. In 2013, over 575,000 acres were consumed by over 1500 fires.
